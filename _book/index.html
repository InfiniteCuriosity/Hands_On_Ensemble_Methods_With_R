1 This book will put the most accurate tools to model nature in your hands

This book will guide you through the entire process of building your own ensemble models from beginning to end. I’ve done my very best to make the book very interesting, fun, and practical. There are lots of examples using real world data with all the steps included.

In the beginning there was a huge blizzard over Chicago. That’s how everything started.

Nature is most accurately described, modeled and predicted using a diverse set of individual models and ensembles of models. Ensembles are the most accurate description of nature we have up to this time. Ensembles are the current best understanding of nature. This book will give you the tools and skills to build a diverse range of individual models and ensembles of models for numeric, classification, logistic and time series data.

I do extreme sports for fun, such as white water rafting, snowboarding, and skydiving, and I’ve never been hurt. The single best way to be safe in extreme sports is to be totally predictable (no surprises!), communicate a plan before you start, and always be ready when things do not go according to plan. This book will follow the same system. This chapter will outline the book, and you’ll have a chance to get a glimpse of some of the highlights of our journey together. Then each chapter will guide you through the way to be the minimize surprises in your work, and we will look at what to do when things do not go as planned.

The goal of predicting and forecasting is not to predict, the goal of forecasting is not to forecast. The goal is to give the team that receives the predictions and forecats the very best preparation for whatever might happen, so they are fully ready ahead of time to respond no matter what happens. That is what success and excellence in predictive analytics looks like. This goal is totally achievable, and this book will help you reach that destination.

That’s why we are doing this. It always helps to know your why.

We are doing this so the team receiving your reports and comments and feedback is as well prepared as they can be for anything that might happen. This is a testable and falsifiable hypothesis.

It’s not important enough to know your why, because there are many whys in any situation. It’s much more valuable to know your absolute rock bottom why. The very foundation on which you stand.

Every why in data science connects to people. No exceptions.
